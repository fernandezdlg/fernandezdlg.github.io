Projects

In this page, you will be able to find a selection of projects I have worked on, and a small description of them.


Master's thesis on Simulations of Lattice Gauge Theories with GPUs (September 2022 - April 2023)

- Worked on an interface to link the the OpenQ*D package with the QUDA library from NVIDIA, to use GPU kernels in our lattice simulations and measurements.
- Successfully demonstrated a proof of concept for accelerating measurements of gauge fields in OpenQ*D using GPUs via QUDA, also achieving efficient scaling.


Partially Coherent Gaussian Beams (August 2020 - June 2021)

- Simulation of partially coherent classical beams with Monte-Carlo methods.
- Study of decoherence in classical mixtures of light modes.
- Classification of partially coherent beams with Neural Networks.
- Work published in SPIE Proceedings (DOI here).


Solitons and Non-linear media (Summer 2019 + August 2020 - April 2021)

- Derivation and characterization of a family of stable Legendre-Lorentzian solitons.
- Article published in the Journal of Optics (DOI here).


Quantum Raman Scattering in a 1D system + environment (Semester project Spring 2020)

- Spontaneous quantum Raman scattering in matter. Simulations of a 1D material with phase-decay mechanisms in the phonon field. For this work, I used the QuTiP package in Python.


Multifractality in the Cryptocurrency Market (January - May 2019)

- Exploration of BTC prices' time-series, and fitting onto a GARCH process. Final project for Econophysics course at NTU.


Computing Neutron Star Properties using General Relativity (January - May 2019)

- Python project that solves numerically the TOV equations for relativistic spherically symmetric static objects. The relativistic results are compared to classical ones. Final project for Computational Physics course at NTU.
